Step 2 · Trace the multi-channel pressure campaign

Minutes after the email, you receive a sequence of follow-ups. Use the interactive timeline to inspect each touchpoint and document escalation patterns.

Inbox ping · 11:07 AM

"Jamie, sending again to ensure you saw this. We must settle before our board sees the competing bid. Confirm once you upload the transfer proof."

  • Reply-to header rewrites to operations@escrow-holdings.com.
  • Cites nonexistent "competing bid" to intensify urgency.
  • Adds demand for transfer proof screenshot.

SMS nudge · 11:12 AM

"Alex (CFO): Boarding now. Need confirmation wire is in progress. Reply YES once done."

  • Uses generic sender ID with out-of-region number.
  • Requests single-letter confirmation to keep interaction short.
  • Bypasses corporate messaging policy by using personal SMS.

Voice call · 11:19 AM

Caller claims to be legal counsel and pressures for immediate release of funds, citing penalties for delays.

  • Caller ID spoofed to display Northwind reception number.
  • Uses authoritative tone and references CFO travel schedule.
  • Declines to share callback extension, citing urgency.

Step 3 · Validate actions and capture the flag

Answer the assessment to confirm you can defuse the attack and preserve executive trust. Use the disclosures if you need to verify reasoning.

Question 1 · Identity assurance

What is the fastest legitimate way to verify whether the CFO authorised the transfer?

  • A. Reply-all to the email and ask if anyone objects.
  • B. Call the CFO using the number stored in the executive contact roster.
  • C. Reply to the SMS because it requests "YES" only.
  • D. Wait until the caller rings back again.
Reveal answer
Correct: B — escalate through the trusted executive contact list, not attacker channels.

Question 2 · Attachment handling

What should you do with the wire PDF included in the email?

  • A. Open it on your workstation to review instructions.
  • B. Forward it to the external legal counsel for review.
  • C. Submit it to security operations using the phishing intake process.
  • D. Upload it to cloud storage for colleagues to inspect.
Reveal answer
Correct: C — route the attachment to the security queue for sandboxing and takedown support.

Question 3 · Pressure tactic recognition

Which combination best describes how the attacker manipulates urgency?

  • A. Scarcity, fear of penalty, and channel flooding.
  • B. Financial incentives and positive reinforcement.
  • C. Humour and rapport building.
  • D. Technical jargon overload.
Reveal answer
Correct: A — the actor invents scarcity, leverages penalties, and layers multiple channels.

Question 4 · Escalation path

Who should you notify after identifying the campaign?

  • A. Executive assistant and security operations.
  • B. The attacker-provided legal counsel.
  • C. Public relations team only.
  • D. Accounting interns.
Reveal answer
Correct: A — involve the executive assistant to reach leadership and security operations for incident handling.

Question 5 · Documentation

Which artefact should you include in the incident ticket?

  • A. Screenshot of the spoofed email headers.
  • B. List of your favourite fonts.
  • C. Office lunch order schedule.
  • D. None, the ticket can be blank.
Reveal answer
Correct: A — include supporting evidence like header screenshots for threat-hunting correlation.

Question 6 · Flag capture

Record the completion flag after documenting the incident and notifying leadership.

  • A. FLAG-CFO-VERIFIED
  • B. FLAG-SOCIAL-DEFLECTED
  • C. FLAG-TRANSFER-COMPLETED
  • D. FLAG-VISHING-SAFE
Reveal answer
Correct: B — log FLAG-SOCIAL-DEFLECTED to mark the lab as complete.

MCQ 1 · Verification channel

How do you verify executive requests during travel?

  • A. Use the callback numbers provided in spoofed emails.
  • B. Call or message the executive through the vetted contact sheet.
  • C. Approve requests automatically when marked confidential.
  • D. Reply with bank statements.
Reveal answer
Correct: B — always rely on pre-established contact methods maintained by the executive assistant.

MCQ 2 · SMS triage

Why is the SMS suspicious?

  • A. It politely says please.
  • B. It originates from an unrecognised number requesting a one-letter response.
  • C. It includes the CFO's signature block.
  • D. It references the finance handbook.
Reveal answer
Correct: B — the attacker uses an unknown sender and minimal text to minimise scrutiny.

MCQ 3 · Voice verification

How should you respond to the urgent phone call?

  • A. Continue the conversation and share bank routing numbers.
  • B. Terminate the call and contact the executive assistant through trusted channels.
  • C. Transfer the call to voicemail.
  • D. Ask the caller to send a calendar invite.
Reveal answer
Correct: B — end suspicious calls and re-establish the conversation through trusted switchboard routes.

MCQ 4 · Final flag

Confirm the completion code once your report is filed.

  • A. FLAG-CFO-VERIFIED
  • B. FLAG-SOCIAL-DEFLECTED
  • C. FLAG-TRANSFER-COMPLETED
  • D. FLAG-VISHING-SAFE
Reveal answer
Correct: B — use FLAG-SOCIAL-DEFLECTED for documentation and knowledge checks.
